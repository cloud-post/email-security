What is DMARC?




DMARC is an acronym for Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance. This protocol is designed to authenticate email messages and prevent fraudulent activities such as phishing attacks and domain spoofing. DMARC allows email domain owners to establish policies that dictate how receiving servers should handle messages that purport to originate from their domain. By deploying DMARC, domain owners can safeguard their email domain's reputation and enhance email deliverability.

  • Analyzing Email Authentication Failures: A DMARC analyzer enables you to obtain comprehensive reports on email authentication failures, enabling you to detect and address potential security risks.
  • Monitoring Email Delivery and Reputation: By utilizing the analyzer, you can gain valuable knowledge regarding your email delivery rates and domain reputation. This knowledge will empower you to uphold a strong deliverability rate and maintain a favorable sender reputation.
  • Enhancing Email Deliverability: Utilizing a DMARC analyzer enables you to enhance your email deliverability and guarantee that authentic emails are successfully delivered to recipients' inboxes, reducing the likelihood of being flagged as spam.
