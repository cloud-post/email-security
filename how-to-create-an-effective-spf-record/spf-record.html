How To Create An Effective SPF Record For Email Marketing

As email marketing becomes more and more commonplace in today's digital world, it's becoming increasingly important to understand how to craft an effective SPF record. An SPF record determines which mail servers are allowed to send emails on behalf of your domain. A properly set up SPF record will help ensure your emails get sent to your inbox, as well as protect your domain from being used for bad purposes. Crafting an effective SPF record can be a challenging task, but with the right knowledge and guidance, it doesn't have to be. 

SPF (Sender Policy Framework) is an authentication protocol designed to verify senders when email addresses are associated with a specific domain. An SPF-protected domain is less vulnerable to spam, which makes it much less likely that it will be blacklisted. SPF further ensures that legitimate email from the domain will be delivered.


Follow the five actions listed on the chart below to set up your SPF:



Track email addresses and activity with IP addresses:

The very first step in determining SPF is to determine which mail servers you use to send email from your domain. Many organizations send mail from varying servers. Create a list of all your mail servers and other IP addresses, and carefully consider whether any of the following are used to send mail that is related to the company:

  • Web hosting service server hosting.
  • Email service provider's (ESP) mail server yours.
  • An in office email server (such as Microsoft Exchange).
  • The mail server of your email provider's users' mailboxes.
  • Other mail servers can be used to send email from your account.

How to make the most of your mailing lists:

It's possible that you have many domains associated with your company. Some of them are often used for email transmission, and others are not.

It's important to ensure SPF records for all your domains, even those that you are not sending from. Why? After you set up SPF protection for your sending domains, the first thing that a criminal will do is try to spoof your non-sending domains.

Draft your SPF record:

SPF authenticates identities by comparing a sending server's IP address to the authorized sending IP addresses the sender publishes in the DNS records from its present domain.

Here's a way to create an SPF record:

To begin, start by attaching the v=spf1(version 1) tag followed by the IP address that is authorized to send email.

To maintain your SPF record valid, you must include an include statement when you send emails from your third-party account.

After you have used all IP addresses and written statements, end your record with a "all" or a "all" comment.

An all tag indicates a soft SPF fail while an all tag indicates a hard SPF fail. In the eyes of the major email providers all and are all both going to result in a SPF failure. Impartial throwing alone means the most protected record.



SPF records can be no more than 255 characters in length and cannot include more than ten include statements.

When your domain does not send email, your SPF record will prohibit any preceding or all bots.

Publish your SPF to the DNS:

Work with your DNS administrator to publish your SPF record to DNS so mailbox providers can reference it.

If you are utilizing a hosting company, such as 123-reg and GoDaddy, or if you are not sure of your ISP's DNS settings, contact your IT department for aid. Email service providers ordinarily submit SPF reports for sending domains by email on behalf of subscribers.

Test it and check if it works:

Verify your email address's SPF record with a SPF checking tool to make sure it looks as expected. You can view what recipients will see: a list of the servers that are authorized to send email on your behalf. If one or more of your sending IP addresses is not listed, you can add it to your account.

In conclusion, creating an effective SPF record for email marketing is an important step for any business. It helps to prevent spoofing, which can damage your reputation and cost you customers. Setting up an SPF record is relatively simple, but making sure your settings are correct is essential for security and avoiding any issues with deliverability. With the right setup, you can be sure that your emails are safe and trusted by your customers.
