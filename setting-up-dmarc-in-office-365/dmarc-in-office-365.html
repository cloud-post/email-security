Step-By-Step Guide To Setting Up DMARC In Office 365


If you are looking for a step-by-step guide to setting up DMARC in Office 365, you have come to the right place. DMARC, which stands for Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting and Conformance, is an important tool for businesses that want to ensure the security of their emails. It ensures that the emails sent from your domain are authentic, and if they are not, they can be blocked or quarantined. 

This is important because it prevents your emails from being marked as spam or phishing attempts, protecting both your reputation and your customers. Setting up DMARC in Office 365 is a fairly straightforward process, and this guide will provide you with all the information you need to do so. From understanding the basics of DMARC to configuring your settings, this guide will provide a comprehensive overview of what you need to do.

DMARC Record:

A DMARC record is a file that exposes your DMARC ruleset. This indicates that it assists you to understand the DMARC question by means of texts, along with how it reports to a recipient in case you are unable to deliver email because of authentication issues.

If you understand how to set up a DMARC record, you effectively decide what must be done regarding an email that has unsuccessfully gone through authentication checks.

Creating a DMARC record can help you fend off cyber-threats such as spoofing, phishing, and CEO fraud as a result of authorized email addresses. To prevent hacking, make sure to accept how to create a DMARC record with DKIM and office 365 SPF.

After generating your DMARC record, follow these simple steps to publish your DMARC record into your Office 365 DNS.



  • Browse the Admin center of the Office 365 portal.

  • Go to the settings screen, choose the domains you want to apply.

  • From the domain choice, you can search, choose, or add desired office 365 domain that you intend to apply to DMARC.

  • If you have selected the desired Office 365 domain, you will be directed to a page advising how to configure your DNS settings for Office 365 services to be displayed.

  • Follow the steps provided by the Office 365 administrator to create a new IP address and domain, then configure the SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records for your new domain. Make sure you utilize the DMARC Record Generator to produce a personalized DMARC record, if necessary.

  • After DMARC is set up, you have to wait for a report before Monday morning so that you can set up DMARC Analyzer. Because most ISPs are sent via DNS-based caching, only one report is sent per day, and two on Saturday or Sunday. Alternately, a DNS bias is used to speed up the process, which can take 72 hours.
