A Comprehensive Guide to VARs

    When it comes to staying ahead of the competition in the digital landscape, having a reliable and knowledgeable technology partner is essential. This is where VARs come in. VARs, or Value Added Resellers, provide a valuable service to organizations by helping them find the best solutions for their technological needs. In this comprehensive guide to VARs, you will discuss everything you need to know about this important partner. Cover the different types of VARs and their roles, the benefits of working with a VAR, and how to choose the right one for your business. With this information, you'll be sure to find the perfect VAR that meets the needs of your organization.


    Value-Added Reseller (VAR):



    A value-added reseller is a business that stitches value-added products and services into third-party products for resale to end users. Value-added resellers play an important role in the information technology industry; they provide additional products, installations services, consulting, troubleshooting, or other related services on top of their core business.


    Successful Strategies for Selling to VARs:

    Value-added vendors play an important role in the distribution channel by providing service to vendors, especially those in the IT industry. A value-added reseller takes the core hardware or software electronic item or system and customizes an asset package of additional equipment devices for a customer.

    The reseller is not the manufacturer of the device, but it is imperative to have a thorough knowledge of the equipment to meet the client's demands and customize the device to meet those demands, install it as requested, test it, and maintain it. An IT corporation will usually promote the establishment of value-added resellers to increase portable application sales.

    Many of these resellers are exclusive to one brand, but many carry several different makes to provide options for customers.

    Value-added resellers transform the already existing value of a product by adding on or customizing its core function so that end users can purchase it again. A value-added reseller encourages repeat customers by offering additional value. Value-added resellers are ill-equipped to control the value of their goods.
